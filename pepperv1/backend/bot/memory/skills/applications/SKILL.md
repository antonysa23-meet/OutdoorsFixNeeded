# Application Skill

Fill out fellowship, accelerator, and grant applications for Adam Towner.

## Rules

1. **NEVER fabricate information.** Only use facts from this skill's knowledge files. If a question asks for something not covered here, leave it blank or flag it for Adam to fill in.
2. **NEVER use em dashes.** Not in any field, essay, or sentence. Use commas, periods, or semicolons instead.
3. **NEVER submit a form.** Fill everything out and leave it for Adam to review. Do not click Submit, Send, or any final submission button.
4. **Use Adam's writing voice.** See "Writing Voice" section at the bottom of `knowledge/narrative-bank.md`. Short declarative sentences. Confident, not hedging. No "I believe" or "I think." Direct and spare. Provocative first-principles thinking. Lead with the contrarian insight, not buried. Draw from the narrative bank's essay sections for personal story content.
5. **Do not exaggerate.** If a claim isn't in the knowledge files, do not invent it. Better to understate than overstate.
6. **Adapt per program.** Match tone to the program. YC wants traction and speed. Thiel wants contrarian vision. EV wants intellectual substance. a16z wants technical depth and market size. Adjust emphasis, not facts.
7. **Check every field twice.** Before finishing, verify: name spelling, email, phone, URLs, dates, equity split, co-founder details all match the facts file exactly.
8. **Flag uncertainty.** If you're unsure about something, type "[CHECK]" in the field so Adam can find and fix it.

### What is Pepper (50 words)
Pepper is an AI agent that learns from scratch. No pre-programmed skills. It taught itself Blender, Salesforce, Anki, music production, and more through a reinforcement learning loop and memory system. 23 people and 2 companies reached out within 24 hours of a demo.

### Core thesis (100 words)
Every company building AI agents is building a central planner. One agent, one objective, one set of instructions. This is the Soviet model applied to software. It works for small tasks the same way central planning works for small economies. It does not scale. The most complex outputs humanity has produced were not designed by a single intelligence. They emerged from incentive structures. The banana supply chain coordinates millions of people across dozens of countries. No one designed this. We are applying this insight to AI: economic environments where populations of agents compete and cooperate through incentive structures.
